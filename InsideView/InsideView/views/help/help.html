How often do I need to do a maintenance check on my AED?

How often do I need to do a maintenance check on my AED?

Perform maintenance checks on your AED(s) with a frequency in line with your organization’s established standards. AED manufacturers require varying frequency of maintenance checks, ranging from daily to monthly, depending on make / model. You can find this information in your AED owner’s manual (see ‘Resources’ tab on your AGS Certification Center to download the appropriate manual if needed). AED laws typically require that the AED is maintained per Manufacturer’s recommendation. AGS Certification requires that AED maintenance checks be performed at least monthly on each AED.

What is included in an AED maintenance check?

What is included in an AED maintenance check?

For each maintenance check, the following items should be verified:
  • Status and service indicator lights, ensuring that they indicate that the AED is in “ready” status and that there are no error or warning messages or tones.
  • Battery expiration date.
  • Electrode pad expiration date
  • No visible damage or wear.
  • Supplies such as extra batteries and rescue ready kit.

Where do I find AED battery expiry date?

Where do I find AED battery expiry date?

Depending on make / model, the AED battery will either contain an expiration date, indicated as such or a date of manufacture. In cases where the date of manufacture is shown, your AED owner’s manual will prescribe a duration for the battery life, and the expiration date will equal the date of manufacture plus the manufacturer's prescribed battery life duration. The battery should be replaced prior to the expiration date. To place and order with AGS to replace your battery you may contact info@AGS.com or 877-88-READY.Additionally, you can order a replacement directly from your AGS Certification Center account by selecting the 'Buy' button next to the relevant AED in the 'AEDs' tab.

How many Trained responders should my facility have?

How many Trained responders should my facility have?

Of course the more people that are AED/CPR trained the better. AGS Certification requires that at least one person who is AED/CPR Certified trained be available for each AED during all hours that a facility is operating. It is also important to also build in redundancy to cover unforeseen absences, breaks, shift changes, etc. As the nature and operations of each organization vary, appropriate numbers of trained responders vary as well. With tools such as our proprietary Measured ReponseTM model, AGS can assist you in selecting an appropriate number of Trained Responders for your organization.

What happens during the AGS AED Inspection?

What happens during the AGS AED Inspection?

The AGS Inspector will contact you before the inspection to schedule a convenient time for the inspection. The inspection time will vary depending on the size of your facility but typically takes one to two hours. Among other things, the Inspector will verify information about the AEDs at your facility and look for recommendations to improve your ability to respond to an SCA event.

What happens after the AGS inspection?

What happens after the AGS inspection?

After the AGS inspection you will receive an Assessment Review which will cohere the information you provided prior to the inspection as well as the inspection results. The Assessment Review will state the requirements met as well as those needed for AGS Certification.

What help does AGS provide after we have experienced an SCA event at our facility?

What help does AGS provide after we have experienced an SCA event at our facility?

AGS provides consultation with the post-incident protocol and should be called within 24 hours of any AED-related incident. AGS includes an on-site visit from one of our trained inspectors following an event for no additional charge. The inspector provides assistance with the information download from the AED, getting the AED back in service, and also provides some qualitative feedback and emotional support based upon his/her professional experience. The inspector will also perform a complimentary full inspection of your facility should you so desire.
